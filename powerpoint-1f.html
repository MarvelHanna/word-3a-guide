Step 2 — 10 pts

Slide Size: Widescreen (16:9)

  1. Click the Design tab in the ribbon.
  2. Far right of the ribbon, click Slide Size.
  3. From the dropdown, click Widescreen (16:9).
  4. If PowerPoint asks how to scale content, pick Maximize (or Ensure Fit if content looks cut off — either is fine for the grader).
All slides should now be wide rectangles instead of the older 4:3 squarer shape.
Step 3 — 10 pts

Outline edit on Slide 2

This step has 4 micro-actions: demote one existing bullet, hit Return, promote, type the new bullet. Read it through once before doing it.

  1. View → Outline View. The slides switch to a text-only outline on the left side.
  2. Find Slide 2 in the outline. Look at the bullets under it. Click anywhere inside the last (bottom) bullet of Slide 2.
  3. On the Home tab, in the Paragraph group, click Decrease Indent (the button with a left-pointing arrow, sometimes labeled Indent Less). The bullet you're on moves one level UP the hierarchy (less indented).
  4. Press the End key (or Fn+Right on Mac) to move the cursor to the end of that bullet's text.
  5. Press Return. A new bullet appears below at the same level.
  6. On the Home tab → Paragraph group, click Increase Indent (right-pointing arrow, Indent More). The new empty bullet moves one level DOWN (more indented).
  7. Now type or paste:
Snorkeling equipment is provided
  1. View → Normal to leave Outline view.
After this, Slide 2's last existing bullet is one indent shallower than before, and the new "Snorkeling equipment is provided" bullet sits under it at the original level.
Step 4 — 6 pts

Slide 2 line spacing 1.5

  1. Still on Slide 2. Click anywhere inside the main content placeholder (the box with the bulleted list).
  2. Cmd+A to select ALL the text in that placeholder.
  3. On the Home tab → Paragraph group, click the Line Spacing button (looks like horizontal lines with up/down arrows) → 1.5.
If Cmd+A only selects one bullet, click once in the placeholder first (so the text cursor blinks inside), then Cmd+A.
Step 5 — 10 pts

Slide 1 layout → Title Slide

  1. In the thumbnails on the left, click Slide 1.
  2. Home tab → Slides group → Layout dropdown.
  3. Pick Title Slide (the layout with one big title and a subtitle below it — usually the first option).
Step 6 — 10 pts

Slide 1 subtitle formatting

Apply 5 changes to the subtitle: Font, Size, Color, Bold, Italic. Do them in any order.

  1. On Slide 1, look at the subtitle placeholder. It contains the text Hikes and Rafting.
  2. Triple-click on Hikes and Rafting to select the entire line (drag-select if triple-click doesn't grab all of it).
  3. Home tab → Font group → click the font name dropdown. Scroll to Cambria and click it.
  4. In the font size box (right of font name), type 24 and press Return.
  5. Click the Font Color arrow (the little dropdown next to the "A" with the color bar). A grid appears with Theme Colors at the top.
  6. In the Theme Colors grid, click the color in column 2, row 1 (top of the second column from the left).
  7. Click Bold (B) — or Cmd+B.
  8. Click Italic (I) — or Cmd+I.
Verify: the subtitle should be in a different color than before, slanted (italic), thicker (bold), and noticeably bigger/different font.
Step 7 — 10 pts

Copy 3 slides from p01F_Slides

Two PowerPoint windows. Copy from one, paste into the other with the destination theme applied.

  1. In Finder, open the assignment folder. Double-click p01F_Slides.pptx — it opens in a separate PowerPoint window.
  2. In that p01F_Slides window, look at the thumbnails on the left. Identify the last 3 slides (the bottom 3).
  3. Click the first of those 3 slides in the thumbnails. Hold Shift and click the last one — all 3 should highlight blue.
  4. Cmd+C to copy.
  5. Switch back to your Hanna_PowerPoint_1F_Hawaii_Tours.pptx window (Cmd+` toggles between windows of the same app).
  6. In the thumbnails, click Slide 3 so it's selected (highlighted).
  7. Right-click (or Control-click) on Slide 3 in the thumbnails. A menu appears.
  8. Find Paste Options in the menu. Three little icons appear. Hover them — pick Use Destination Theme (the one that matches your current Berlin/Hawaii theme, NOT the Keep Source Formatting one).
After paste, your presentation has 6 slides: 1 (Title), 2 (Bullets, edited), 3 (Starter Slide 3), 4 (pasted #1), 5 (pasted #2), 6 (pasted #3).
Step 8 — 10 pts

Slide 4: center title and caption

  1. In thumbnails, click Slide 4 (the first slide you pasted in).
  2. Slide 4 has a small picture with text next to it — a "Caption" layout. It has two text areas: a title placeholder and a caption/text placeholder.
  3. Click inside the title placeholder (the larger/bolder text area). Cmd+A to select all. Cmd+E to center.
  4. Click inside the caption placeholder (the body text next to the image). Cmd+A. Cmd+E.
If you can't tell which is the title vs caption, look at the font size — title is usually bigger.
Step 9 — 8 pts

Slide Sorter: delete Slide 3, move new Slide 3 to after Slide 1

Two operations. After the delete, what was Slide 4 becomes the new Slide 3 — that's the one you move.

  1. View → Slide Sorter. All slides display as thumbnails in a grid.
  2. Right-click Slide 3 (the original starter slide, NOT the pasted one) → Delete Slide.
  3. The pasted slide that was Slide 4 is now Slide 3 (everything shifted up by one). Click that new Slide 3 to select it.
  4. Drag it left and up to drop it between Slide 1 and Slide 2. You should see a vertical line cursor appear between them — release there.
  5. It's now the new Slide 2. The slide that was Slide 2 (bullets) is now Slide 3.
  6. View → Normal.
Step 10 — 6 pts

Move Slide 4 to the end

  1. In the thumbnails (Normal view is fine, or back to Slide Sorter), click Slide 4.
  2. Drag it down past the last slide and release. Slide 4 is now at the end (last position).
Final deck should be 5 slides. Order: Title → (pasted caption) → Bullets (edited) → (pasted) → (pasted, was Slide 4).
Step 11 — 10 pts

Split transition, Horizontal Out, 1.75s, all slides

  1. In thumbnails, click Slide 1.
  2. Click the Transitions tab in the ribbon.
  3. In the transition gallery, find and click Split (use the ScreenTip / hover to confirm the name).
  4. Click Effect Options (to the right of the gallery). Choose Horizontal Out.
  5. In the Timing group (right side of the Transitions tab), find Duration. Click in the box and set it to 01.75 (or just 1.75).
  6. Click Apply To All in the Timing group.
Verify: in the thumbnails, each slide should have a small star icon ⭐ indicating it has a transition.
Step 12 — 10 pts

Header & Footer on Notes and Handouts

  1. Insert tab → Text group → Header & Footer.
  2. In the dialog, click the Notes and Handouts tab (NOT the Slide tab).
  3. Check Date and time. Make sure the Update automatically radio button is selected (not "Fixed").
  4. Check Page number.
  5. Uncheck Header if it's checked (the instruction doesn't mention a header).
  6. Check Footer. Click in the Footer text box and type or paste this exactly (underscore, not space):
1F_Hawaii_Tours
  1. Click Apply to All (NOT just "Apply").
Done
